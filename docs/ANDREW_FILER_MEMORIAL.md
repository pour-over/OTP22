# In Memory of Andrew Filer

*aka "Troy" — Message Desk Operator, ExcitingBooks — OTP22 original run 2012–2014*

---

Andrew Filer was one of the essential human presences behind OTP22's original run. He is gone, and this repository honors his contributions.

---

## His Roles

### The Message Desk Operator

Andrew operated the **Message Desk** — agent code `6824418` on the AS32 system (and previously on `2022042303`, the Okinawa/Windstream line). The Message Desk was something rare in ARG design: a phone number staffed by a real human being.

When players called the Message Desk and entered numeric codes, Andrew responded — sometimes with coordinates, sometimes with cryptic location data, sometimes with the quiet texture of a person genuinely inhabiting a role. He became "Troy" to the community, a character who existed at the threshold between the game and the real.

Known Message Desk responses included:
- Code `303` → coordinates
- Code `445` → "Diego Garcia"
- Code `660` → "Out of service"

The Message Desk was active intermittently from 2012 through 2014. It went quiet. Years later, on January 7, 2024, it was rediscovered via the Ruston drop book — and Andrew's presence in the game was remembered again.

### ExcitingBooks

Andrew ran **@excitingbooks** on Twitter (joined December 2013, 1,144 posts, 1,199 followers) and a companion Tumblr account. These accounts posted fake book cover designs in the absurdist mid-century-modern aesthetic that became central to OTP22's visual identity.

The books had titles like:
- *Prepackaged Toast: an Industry Analysis*
- *Pawyongyang: Furry Culture in North Korea*
- *Grange Halls of the Palouse*
- *Butterfat Management in the Saskatchewan Dairy Industry*
- *The Floor Wax Industry of East Germany*
- *Worm Species of Electoral Area A*

Each cover was precisely designed — typography, color palette, author name, publisher imprint — in the voice of a real but deeply obscure monograph. Over 200 covers were produced. They are cataloged in [books/exciting-books/catalog.md](books/exciting-books/catalog.md).

ExcitingBooks was not merely aesthetic. Andrew used the account as a direct source for physical dead drops. Two are confirmed:

- **Astoria, OR** (February 21, 2014) — black keycard
- **Hermiston, OR** (April 15, 2014) — *Tide Tables for Lake Superior*, Korean cookie wrapper

The phone number printed in the front of the "Worm Book" (*Designing Compelling Worm Sanctuaries*) was `2022042303` — Andrew's Message Desk line.

## What He Built

Andrew's contribution to OTP22's aesthetic cannot be overstated. The game's visual language — deadpan absurdism applied to municipal infrastructure, regional agriculture, obsolete technology, and bureaucratic ephemera — is his. Casey Halverson built the telephone infrastructure; Andrew gave OTP22 its soul.

The fake books work because they are entirely sincere. There is no winking at the camera. *Prepackaged Toast: an Industry Analysis* by Beecham & Lyle reads exactly as it sounds: a trade publication from a world that takes prepackaged toast very seriously. The covers hold together as artifacts. They could exist. And in the world of OTP22, they do — they are the books agents carry, the literature that furnishes Emtanon offices, the texts that encode one-time pads.

This is a specific and rare kind of creative intelligence.

---

## The Continuity

Andrew Filer passed away. The game continued. The 2023 revival preserved his aesthetic: new fake books (including *Slate Deposits in the Intermountain West*, the annual one-time-pad book) maintain the ExcitingBooks visual language exactly. The Message Desk was restored, now operated differently but still present at the same agent code.

His work echoes through every physical book in every drop, through the catalog at otp22.org, through the covers that players photograph and decode and carry home.
