## Gough Ceremony

**Source:** https://otp22.org/doku.php?id=events:gough_ceremony
**Date:** September 24, 2023, 15:00 UTC
**Location:** Gough Island
**Audio:** [gough_ceremony_feat._chris_moron.ogg](https://otp22.org/lib/exe/fetch.php?media=events:gough_ceremony_feat._chris_moron.ogg)

The Gough Ceremony was broadcast over **all agent systems simultaneously**, rendering them unusable for regular communication with WORM agents for approximately 20 minutes.

### Program

1. Dramatic helicopter entry by Dr. J Marvin Blackweather and his companion (Tala Torres)
2. **Memorial of the MELTR3 incident** and the Delaware Tape Retrieval System fire
   - One minute of silence
   - 33-gun salute
   - SSTV images of MELTR3 crew broadcast during memorial
3. Link-up to a sentencing hearing aboard FACILITY prison ship
4. **Surprise enlistment** of Gough Island interns into the Marine Org

### Disruptions

- Start delayed by several minutes
- Connection dropped unexpectedly 16 minutes in
- J's grand speech cut short by the **South African Navy**, who arrived for a surprise audit of the weather station

### The MELTR3 Incident

MELTR3 was a Melter fleet vessel lost in an unspecified incident before the revival era. The memorial ceremony and SSTV images of the crew are the primary record. This connects to the original-run SHIP fleet (MELTER1, MELTER2, MELTER3 tracked in Arctic waters).

## Operation Spice

**Source:** https://otp22.org/doku.php?id=events:operation_spice
**Date:** January 2024
**Location:** Lima, Peru

Operation Spice was a **failed WORM operation** aimed at capturing and detaining counter-operatives from a local hackerspace in Lima, Peru.

### Course of the Operation

Preparations for the operation in Lima were made in late 2023. The operation aimed to neutralize players who had been successfully intercepting WORM's dead drops.

### What Went Wrong

Due to mismanagement and several organizational mishaps:
- WORM agents were captured by the **Peruvian cartel** instead of accomplishing their objective
- The community interfered, further disrupting the operation

### Aftermath

- **MO3 lost** — the ship was lost as a direct consequence of Operation Spice's failure
- **MO5 crew defected** — crew turned against WORM command
- The ship formerly designated MO3 was replaced; MO5 took its Iris talk group `201`

## FACILITY Redemption

**Source:** https://otp22.org/doku.php?id=events:facility_redemption
**Date:** February 10, 2024

FACILITY was an old cargo ship retrofitted as a **prison** for "counter operation" and disobedient Emtanon employees. Threats of detainment were common throughout 2023. FACILITY had a resident "judge" who gave out sentences in "trials that were certainly fair."

### How It Happened

1. **Jeff A.** provided the community with passwords to FACILITY's semaphores
2. On February 10, 2024, players found a semaphore filled with `0xFF` bytes
3. Players flipped them to `0x00`, not knowing what it would do
4. **All prisoners were unlocked** — every detainee on the ship was freed simultaneously

### Aftermath

- FACILITY was subsequently **lost** — the freed prisoners took over and the ship became unaccountable to WORM command
- Listed as "Former" in Marine Org's asset roster
- Represents one of the most direct player interventions in the OTP22 narrative

### Significance

The FACILITY Redemption demonstrates the community's ability to directly affect the in-world narrative. Jeff's decision to share passwords with players — despite personal risk — mirrors the original run's "Robby" whistleblower dynamic.
