---

## Notes on This Collection

Andrew Filer created 200+ absurdist fake book covers with deadpan mid-century modern graphic design. Titles obsessively reference real obscure places, defunct industries, and arcane technical standards — exactly the kind of ephemera that no one would imagine faking. Categories include:

- **Telecommunications:** Telephone exchange buildings, numbering plans, plugs, switches
- **Agriculture/Food:** Farming, grain elevators, vegetables, dairy, bees
- **Municipal infrastructure:** Sewers, roads, parking, water meters, bus shelters
- **Vintage computing:** ALGOL, BASIC, FOCAL, Sinclair, Acorn, MUMPS
- **Eastern European industry:** Soviet planning, DDR industry, Yugoslav manufacturing
- **Natural history:** Fungi, algae, parasites, ferns, lichens
- **Regional minutiae:** County-level topics, township roads, rural electrification
- **The game itself:** "Designing Compelling Worm Sanctuaries" is the pivotal one

Physical books with OTP22 content were printed using these cover designs and placed in drops.

## Game-Significant Titles

These titles have direct connections to game content:

| Title | Significance |
|-------|-------------|
| **Designing Compelling Worm Sanctuaries** | The primary physical drop book. Multiple editions (#1–3) found in Kauai, Wallace ID, Sofia Bulgaria. Contains OTP keys and operational data. Author: North American Helminthological Society. |
| **Slate Deposits in the Intermountain West** | OTP key book for Slate messages on AS32. 365 numbered rows × 32 numbers × 3 sections. Annual editions distributed via postal mail. |
| **Global Response Codes** | Physical book authored (in-game) by "Dr. J. Marvin Blackweather." Found in Nadando drop (Tempe, AZ). First physical mention of J. |
| **Worldwide Phone Book Typefaces** | Contains numbers with utility noted as "doubtful but it's data." |
| **Molds and Lichens of Nova Scotia** | Found at Rainier #2 drop with handwritten: +1 709 700 0122 and L26037183A |
