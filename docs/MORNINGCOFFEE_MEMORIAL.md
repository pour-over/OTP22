# In Memory of Morningcoffee

*— passed 2025*

---

There are people who find a community, and there are people who *become* the reason a community keeps going.

Morningcoffee was the second kind.

She showed up for this strange, sprawling, maddening thing — phone numbers that rang in the middle of the night, dead drops in parking lots, messages that needed to be decoded before anyone else could understand what was happening — and she stayed. She brought warmth to something that could have been cold, humor to something that was often frustrating, and persistence to something that asked a lot of the people who cared about it.

The puzzles were hard. The silences between drops were long. There were stretches when it wasn't clear anything was still running at all. A lot of people drifted away during those stretches.

She didn't.

That mattered more than she probably knew. When you could see that someone was still paying attention, still showing up, still curious — it made it easier to stay curious yourself. The community stayed alive in part because she was there keeping it alive.

She passed in 2025. The archive she helped sustain is still here.
