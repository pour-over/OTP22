# Community History

*How the OTP22 player community formed, evolved, and persisted.*

---

## Origins (2011–2012)

OTP22 began without a player community — just a phone number posted on a conspiracy forum.

**November 12, 2011**: User "reaperr" posts about `303-309-0004` on GodlikeProductions. Initial discussion. The number is strange. People call it. Not much traction.

**September 10, 2012**: A 4chan /x/ thread ("Creepy Phone Numbers") rediscovers the number. /x/ — the "paranormal" board — was the natural home for strange phone numbers in 2012. This post caused the community to coalesce for the first time.

Within days:
- People are calling systematically and logging results
- The agent code structure becomes apparent
- The 303.sashahart.net research wiki begins

**September 14, 2012**: First physical drop found — Lake Oswego, OR. The community suddenly realizes this is not just a phone curiosity; it's a game with physical objects in the real world. This changes everything.

---

## The Research Wiki (303.sashahart.net)

The original community organized primarily through **303.sashahart.net** — a research wiki/database built and maintained by the community. It tracked:
- Phone numbers and their behaviors
- Agent code call logs (thousands of logged calls)
- Drop events and their contents
- Decoded messages
- Physical objects inventory
- Data files from drops

This wiki became the authoritative reference for the original run. The site at `303.sashahart.net` remains accessible (though its last update was circa 2015).

The referata wiki (`otp22.referata.com`) was a parallel community resource, now offline.

---

## IRC (#otp22)

The community had an active IRC channel: `#otp22`. IRC logs from the active period are archived at:
http://198.23.165.56/irclogs/%23otp22/

Real-time coordination of drop retrieval, message decoding, and theory-building happened here. This is where players like L0j1k, drawbars, Smugtrio, Blyind, Nadando, Braco, TonyInICT, and others coordinated.

---

## Unfiction (2012–2015)

**Unfiction** (forums.unfiction.com) was the premier ARG research community in the 2010s. OTP22 had a dedicated thread: http://forums.unfiction.com/forums/viewtopic.php?t=36166

Unfiction brought a more structured ARG-research mindset to the game, complementing the more organic 4chan/IRC approach.

- **L0j1k**: The most prolific drop retriever. Held the Henderson NV Iridium SIM, the MLPL OBJECTIVES document, the Bulgaria CD, multiple Kauai CDs, the Worm Sanctuaries books, and more.

## The Dark Period (2016–2023)

The game went quiet in 2016. The player community dispersed. The referata wiki went offline. The sashahart.net database remained accessible but was no longer updated.

For seven years, OTP22 existed primarily as a memory and as archived community resources.

---

## Revival Discovery (May 2023)

When the revival launched, it was primarily discovered through r/OTP22 on Reddit. Casey Halverson (u/tanik) made posts indicating the game was back.

The revival brought new players who had never experienced the original run alongside veteran players returning from the dark period. The community now operates primarily through:
- **r/OTP22** on Reddit
- The **otp22.org** wiki (current community resource)
- An **OTP22 Discord server**

---

## The otp22.org Wiki (2023–present)

The current community wiki at **otp22.org** was built for the revival. It maintains:
- Full documentation of the revival-era game
- Some original run documentation migrated from sashahart.net
- Community tools at otp22.org/tools/
- Drop documentation, message archives, system documentation

## Cultural Legacy

OTP22's player community developed real skills:
- XOR decryption and one-time pad cryptography
- SSTV reception and decoding
- ITA2/Baudot teletype decoding
- PSK31 and other HF digital mode reception
- Custom audio protocol analysis (ABISM, TAMI, M6, M7)
- Steganography extraction (Steghide)
- Physical surveillance and dead drop retrieval
- International coordination for drop pickup

For an ARG that started on a conspiracy forum, OTP22 produced a technically sophisticated community.
